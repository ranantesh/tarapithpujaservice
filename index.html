Welcome To Tarapith

Tara Maa, also known as Goddess Tara, holds a significant place in Hindu and Buddhist traditions as a revered deity embodying compassion, protection, and guidance. In Hinduism, Tara is considered one of the forms of the divine feminine energy, and she is often associated with the fierce aspect of Goddess Parvati. In Tibetan Buddhism, Tara is venerated as a Bodhisattva, a compassionate being who has attained enlightenment but chooses to remain in the cycle of birth and death to help all sentient beings. Tara is depicted in various forms, each representing a different aspect of her compassionate nature. Commonly depicted with a serene countenance, adorned with jewels and seated on a lotus, Tara is a symbol of strength, wisdom, and unconditional love. Devotees turn to Tara Maa for protection, guidance, and the alleviation of suffering, believing in her ability to swiftly respond to their prayers and extend her benevolent grace.
